<p>Mapping Color in History is a digital humanities project that brings together the scientific data drawn from existing and ongoing material analyses of pigments in Asian painting in a historical perspective. Funding sources include the National Endowment for the Humanities, the Harvard Dean&#39;s Competitive Fund for Promising Scholarship, and the Harvard Barajas Dean&#39;s Innovation Fund for Digital Arts and Humanities.</p><p>Dr. Jinah Kim is the project PI. I am Digital Technology Coordinator for the project, maintaining the project infrastructure, testing features, providing feedback, designing D3 / Leaflet visualizations, and occasionally doing fullstack work in Django. MCiH is in active development, anticipating a public release in 2021.</p>
